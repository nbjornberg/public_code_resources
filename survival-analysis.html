Survival analysis (time to event)

An analysis technique to model time-associated binary, non-repeated outcomes. Survival analyses are especially useful at controlling for censoring (missing data) that would otherwise bias the estimates.

Common methods: - Kaplan-Meier estimates for the survival rate - Logrank tests to compare survival curves - Cox proportional hazard regression model for statistical significance testing

General data format: Event (0/1) | Time (e.g, 10, 20, 20+)

Probabilities modeled: 1. survival - probability that an individual survived from the time origin to a specific future time (event not occurring) 2. hazard - probability that an individual who has survived to time t has an event (event occurring)
